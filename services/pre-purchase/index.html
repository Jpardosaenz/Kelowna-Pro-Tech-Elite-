FAQ

What do you inspect during a pre-purchase inspection?

We run a full dealer-level scan with an Autel diagnostic tool to pull live data and stored fault codes, then physically inspect the engine, transmission, brakes, suspension, and frame for damage or wear a visual walk-around would miss. We also check tires, fluids, and leaks, and do a road test when the seller allows it. Everything is organized into 10 systems with 100+ individual checks, ranked from the risk that costs you the most (engine, transmission, frame, brakes) to routine verification (tires, road test). You get a photo-rich report the same day, before you decide.

Do I have to bring the car to you, or do you come to me?

We come to you. That is the whole point of a mobile pre-purchase inspection: you never tow or drive an unknown vehicle anywhere before it has been checked. We meet you at the seller's driveway, the dealer lot, or wherever the car currently is, anywhere in Kelowna, West Kelowna, Vernon, Lake Country, Peachland, or Penticton. Most competing inspection services still expect you to bring the vehicle to a shop; we built this service specifically so you never have to.

How much can I lose if I skip a pre-purchase inspection?

A hidden engine or transmission problem on a used vehicle can run $3,000 to $10,000 or more to fix once you already own the car, since major mechanical failures are exactly what a seller's clean CarFax and visual walk-around won't show you. A pre-purchase inspection is often less than 1% of the car's price and takes about an hour, before you own the problem. We've caught pending fault codes a dealer cleared before a sale, worn brake rotors below spec, and suspension damage that a "certified pre-owned" sticker didn't disclose.

How fast can you schedule an inspection?

Same-day is often possible in Kelowna and the surrounding Okanagan Valley. Call or text +1-250-859-5467 with the vehicle's location and we'll confirm the soonest available slot. Because we're mobile, there's no shop queue to wait behind — the inspection happens wherever the car already is, which is usually the biggest time saver compared to booking a shop-based inspection days out.

What's included in the report I get?

Your report is delivered on-site, right after the inspection is finished, not emailed days later. It includes real-time photos taken during the inspection, a repair suggestion and estimated cost for every finding, and green/amber/red risk ratings so you can see at a glance what's urgent versus what's routine maintenance. If you ask for it, we also add a negotiation-ready summary you can hand directly to the seller or dealer to request repairs or a price adjustment before you close.

Do you inspect vehicles at a dealer lot, or only private sellers?

Both. We regularly meet clients at dealer lots across Kelowna as well as private seller driveways, workplaces, and storage lots. A "certified pre-owned" badge or a dealer's own inspection sticker does not replace an independent, dealer-level scan — dealers have an incentive to sell the car, we don't. We've found pending fault codes a dealer cleared before a sale, so an independent inspection matters just as much, if not more, when buying from a dealer.
