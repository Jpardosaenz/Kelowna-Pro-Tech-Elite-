He called on a Tuesday morning. His red BMW Z3 — a classic M roadster — had stopped moving in a commercial parking lot near Harvey Avenue.

Coolant was dripping onto the asphalt. He'd called a tow company. The quote was only to move it to the nearest shop, where he'd wait days for a diagnosis.

He found Kelowna Protech Elite instead.

What the Owner Expected

Joseph arrived at the lot within hours. No flatbed. No shop. Just a mechanic with 15 years of diagnostic experience and professional-grade Autel equipment — the same tools dealers use.

What the owner thought: a coolant leak, probably a hose. A simple fix, maybe a small repair.

What the Autel found: a different story entirely.

Findings — Full Vehicle Assessment

  • Cracked vacuum line running along the engine bay — brittle from age and heat cycles, causing the visible coolant leak
  • Front tire worn completely through wear indicators — not "getting low," but gone. A blowout waiting to happen at highway speed on Highway 97
  • Additional fault codes pointing to deferred maintenance that had been quietly building for months

A Blowout Waiting to Happen

That front tire wasn't "getting low" — it was gone. A blowout waiting to happen at highway speed. The kind of failure that doesn't give you a second chance on Highway 97.

The engine scan added more to the picture: fault codes pointing to deferred maintenance that had been quietly building for months — the kind of thing shops miss when they're only looking at the complaint, not the car.

No Tow. No Waiting Room. No "We'll Call You"

The owner stood in the parking lot and got the full picture — not a phone call from a service advisor three days later, not a surprise invoice. Right there, in plain language, with photos of every finding.

He made an informed decision about his car the same morning it broke down.

That's the difference.
